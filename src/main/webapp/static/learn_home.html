How Does Gerrymandering Affect You?

Gerrymandering Explained

The ways in which a respresentative is voted for inherently depend on how the district lines are designed. When these lines are changed, then the sample of voters voting for that representative may change as well. This pattern has led to many attempts to try and guide the ways in which certain voters reside in particular districts, and thereby affecting how many votes the representative for that area will receive. Despite being a tactic that has been notably seen in recent elections, gerrymandering has been used in political matters since the creation of the Constitution, as a result of Patrick Henry's attempt to deny a congressional seat to James Madison. To be specific, Henry also tried to design the district lines for Madison's area so that many of the voters that reside in that district would oppose Madison. Despite this failed attempt, this method has been utilized ever since to try and gain the upperhand in political elections.

Test Analysis of Gerrymandering

The tests used for testing in regards to Gerrymandering consist of four commonly used methods. The first is the lopsided wins test which can be considered similar to the the two-sample t-test. The second is the consistent advantage test, which depends on the difference between the mean and median of the winning party's vote percentage within a state. The third is the excess seats test, which compares the winning party's won seats in a state to the national mean of seats that the winning party is expected to win via simulations.

Legal Efforts

Current Partisan Gerrymandering Cases

Recently, there have been multiple instances where attempts of gerrymandering certain districts across the United States have been under scrutiny as various parties, and even the Supreme Court, have taken notice of their effects. Cases like "Benisek v. Lamone" and "Harris v. Cooper" have thereby led to the outcome of challenging the winning party in states like North Carolina and Maryland where gerrymandering efforts have been notably consistent.
